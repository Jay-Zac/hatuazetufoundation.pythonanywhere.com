1. Udada na Sanaa Program: Community Education and Awareness Creation

We create a safe and inclusive space for all our members to share their stories, experiences, and ideas. Through our efforts, we continue to create a strong network and supportive environment for us all to grow, change, and create long-lasting positive impacts on our community through art. This program harnesses the power of art as a transformative tool to educate the public and challenge oppressive notions and systems that undermine women and girls.

Through a blend of visual, performing, and digital arts, this initiative aims to spark critical strong conversations about gender equality, empowering women, and dismantling harmful stereotypes. We not only showcase the resilience and achievements of women and girls but also expose the deep-seated biases and injustices they face. By engaging diverse audiences through artistic expression, this program seeks to inspire empathy, awareness, and activism, ultimately fostering a society where gender equality is not just a dream but a lived reality.

2. Wezesha Program: Inclusive Education for Autistic Children

This program is a comprehensive initiative designed to empower and support autistic young people in multiple facets of their lives. It encompasses a holistic approach, combining educational programs tailored to their specific needs, a well-balanced feeding program to ensure their nutritional well-being, robust measures to protect them from violence and exploitation, and comprehensive sexual health and rights education to promote their autonomy and safety.

Additionally, it provides essential guidance and resources to parents, helping them improve their livelihoods and become better advocates for their children. Moreover, this program aims to transform societal perceptions and attitudes towards children with disabilities, fostering inclusion and understanding within communities, ultimately creating a more inclusive and accepting environment for these exceptional young individuals. Our goal is to create an environment where every child can learn and grow.

4. Climate Justice

This program is dedicated to elevating the voices of women and girls in our communities who bear the heaviest burdens of climate change impacts, yet have made the smallest contributions to the climate crisis. Specifically, we create spaces for educating communities on the most effective solutions that begin with safeguarding their land, waterways, and other vital natural resources. We nurture creators and advocates on innovative climate solutions including protecting and disseminating indigenous wisdom and policy advocacy.
